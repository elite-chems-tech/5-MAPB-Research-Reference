pharmacology and forensic toxicology analytical panels.

**CAS:** 1354631-24-5 | **MW:** 189.25 g/mol | **Formula:** C₁₂H₁₅NO | 
**Form:** Crystalline powder (HCl salt) | **Appearance:** White to off-white

---

**GC-MS:** Molecular ion at m/z 189. Base peak at m/z 58 
(N-methylaminopropyl fragment) — characteristic of benzofuran-class 
compounds. If molecular ion appears at m/z 219, the compound is 
5-MeO-MAPB not 5-MAPB — the 30 Da difference confirms methoxy 
substitution at the 5-position.

**HPLC:** UV absorption at λmax ~220–230 nm on C18 reverse-phase column 
with acetonitrile/water gradient. Baseline separation from 5-MeO-MAPB 
achievable under standard conditions.

**NMR:** Absence of singlet at δ 3.85 ppm in ¹H NMR (CDCl₃) confirms 
5-MAPB identity vs 5-MeO-MAPB — the δ 3.85 ppm signal is diagnostic 
for the methoxy group in the latter compound only.

**Storage:** -20°C long-term in amber glass with desiccant. HCl salt 
is hygroscopic — minimize open-container time. UV photodegradation 
susceptibility — protect from direct light. Short-term bench use at 
≤25°C acceptable for up to 2 weeks when properly sealed.

**Solubility:** DMSO (≥10 mg/mL), methanol (soluble), ethanol absolute 
(soluble), distilled water (soluble), acetonitrile (sparingly soluble). 
Final DMSO concentration in assay media should not exceed 0.1%.
